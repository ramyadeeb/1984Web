Dear Investor,

As the TikTok drama unfolds, it's easy to write off the Trump administration's threat to shut off the video app as yet another episode in the US-China saga. But a deeper look reveals a broader trend. In June, following a border skirmish with next-door China, India retaliated in part by shutting off TikTok along with 58 other Chinese apps. And in July Slack decided to pursue antitrust action against Microsoft over the bundling of its Teams product not in the US, home to both companies, but rather in the European Union where regulators look more harshly on tech giants.

These stories may seem like disparate anecdotes, but they highlight a shared underpinning: the internet is becoming fragmented. A combination of government and company politics are creating four disparate internet models across the world: a US internet marked by freewheeling and innovation, a robust Chinese ecosystem behind a closed off wall, a European one hampered by regulation, and an emerging Indian model dominated by Jio that may soon be followed in other developing countries.

This fragmentation may serve various political interests but is costly to businesses and consumers. It is the subject of our newsletter for this month.

In July we made a new investment in Zero Grocery, the startup upending grocery delivery by cutting out middlemen and offering plastic free packaging. Also, our portfolio company Shelf Engine announced its Series A round led by GGV.

Without further ado, here's your newsletter for August.

Sincerely,

3. From Minecraft Tricks to Twitter Hack: A Florida Teen’s Troubled Online Path

On July 15th Twitter suffered its biggest security lapse to date when a hacker took control of 120 accounts, including those of Joe Biden and Elon Musk, and used a cryptocurrency scam to collect $180K. The hacker was able to access Twitter's internal admin tools though a two-step process: he first gained access to Twitter employees’ accounts using phone phishing. (Twitter has been frustratingly opaque on the details.) And then used these employees’ credentials to gain access to other accounts with admin tool access.
