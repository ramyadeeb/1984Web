1. Why Everybody Is Freaking Out About Political Ads on Facebook and Google

In response to the media onslaught on microtargeting, Twitter has banned all political ads (in what's effectively a PR stunt), while Google has banned 'Audience Targeting.' But are we having the right conversation? This thoughtful article from Vox sums up the variety of targeting techniques used by different media players. It concludes that the chatter about political advertising distracts from much bigger and more complicated problems: namely, the organic, not paid, content that often spreads disinformation. 2. Tech and Liberty

With quotes from John Stuart Mill, the Federalists Papers, and some Supreme Court briefs, Ben Thompson jumps to the defense of Facebook with a simple message: "The original justification for the First Amendment was to avoid tyranny, and Facebook deciding what is or is not true would be exactly that—tyranny." He goes a little further and brings up a different type of tyranny, that of the prevailing opinion: "The media is so up in arms against Facebook that I find it deeply concerning that I might have any trepidation in writing that Facebook made the right decision". He leaves with a scathing message: "Tech specifically and the U.S. generally needs more free thinkers, not fewer. Mob mentalities, no matter their good intentions, leave little room for freedom of thought, and lots of room for the status quo." Hats off. Read Article →

3. Fake News in AI - How To Recognize AI Snake Oil

In this short slide presentation, Princeton CS professor Narayanan breaks down the hype vs reality in AI. He separates AI applications into three areas: perception (image and voice recognition) where AI has made great strides, automating judgment (spam detection and content recommendations) where AI works well but is far from perfect, and, last, predicting social outcomes where AI is all hype and the outcomes are highly dubious.
